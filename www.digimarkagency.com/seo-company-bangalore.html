The 10-criteria evaluation framework

Score each agency from 1–5 on every criterion. A reputable Bangalore SEO partner should score 4 or higher on at least eight of ten.

1. Years delivering retainers

Not company age — ask how many active retainer engagements they run today, and for how long.

2. Technical SEO depth

In-house ability to fix crawl, indexation, Core Web Vitals and schema — not just publish blogs.

3. Reporting transparency

Live Search Console and GA4 dashboards with clear monthly action plans, not screenshots.

4. Verifiable case studies

Real results you can check — named clients or analytics evidence, not vanity claims.

5. Local Bangalore experience

Demonstrated Map Pack and Google Business Profile results in the local market.

6. A named, reachable team

A specific SEO lead you can meet — not “our team will handle it.”

7. Ethical link building

Editorial links and digital PR, never bought links or PBNs that risk penalties.

8. Conversion focus

Growth in non-branded organic traffic and qualified leads — rankings without conversions is vanity.

9. Business-outcome reporting

Reports that map keyword movement to traffic and enquiries, not just impressions.

10. Contract flexibility

3–6 month terms with quarterly exit clauses. 12-month lock-ins on unproven scope protect the agency, not you.

The 7 questions to ask every agency on your shortlist

These filter out most weak vendors in under thirty minutes. Take notes — the gap between rehearsed sales talk and real operational depth shows quickly.

  • 1. Who specifically will work on my account day-to-day? (Ideal: a named lead and team you can meet.)
  • 2. Can you show me two active client dashboards? (Ideal: live reporting, not slides.)
  • 3. What is your monthly reporting cadence and format?
  • 4. Can I exit the contract after 90 days if it is not working?
  • 5. How do you handle Google algorithm updates?
  • 6. What link-building methods do you actually use?
  • 7. What happens if rankings drop — what is your response?

Then apply a simple test: search for the specialties an agency claims — technical SEO, ecommerce SEO, local SEO in Bangalore. If they rank for none of their own claimed strengths, treat the claim as marketing, not capability.

Contract red flags — review every clause

Most disputes trace back to the contract, not the SEO. Before you sign, check for:

  • 12-month-plus lock-ins on unproven scope — healthy terms are 3–6 months with quarterly exits.
  • Ownership clauses on the backlinks and content you paid for.
  • Vague monthly deliverable scope with no defined outputs.
  • No clear exit clause, or punitive early-termination fees.
  • Performance-bonus tiers tied to specific ranking positions — an incentive to chase risky shortcuts.
  • Hidden add-on fees for reporting, audits or “extra” work.
  • Missing service-level commitments — no stated cadence, response times or accountability.

And the universal one: any guarantee of specific rankings. Google states no agency can guarantee positions. “Guaranteed #1 in 30 days” signals either dishonesty or risky techniques that invite penalties.
