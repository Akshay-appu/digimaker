What is AI SEO

AI SEO: optimising to be the answer, not just a link

AI SEO is the practice of structuring your website and content so AI-powered search experiences can retrieve, understand, cite and recommend your brand. It extends traditional SEO rather than replacing it — Google's own 2026 guidance is clear that optimising for generative AI "is still SEO." The difference is the goal: instead of only ranking among ten blue links, you work to be one of the handful of sources an AI answer synthesises and names.

Search behaviour in India and worldwide is shifting fast. People increasingly ask Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Gemini and Perplexity a full question and read a synthesised answer that cites only a few trusted sources. If your business is not one of those sources, you are invisible in that moment — even if you rank on page one of classic results. AI SEO closes that gap while protecting and strengthening the organic rankings you already have.

Terminology, without the hype

GEO vs. AEO vs. LLMO explained

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) means optimising to be cited and summarised inside generative AI answers — the term comes from a 2023 Princeton research paper. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) means optimising to be the direct answer in featured snippets, voice results and AI answer boxes. LLMO refers to optimising for large language models generally. In practice these overlap heavily and are used almost interchangeably in 2026 — we focus on the outcome (being the trusted, cited source) rather than debating labels.

How does retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) affect SEO?

Most AI search engines use retrieval-augmented generation (RAG): they search an index, retrieve the most relevant passages, and generate the answer from those passages — citing the sources they used. LLM optimisation means structuring your pages so your passages are the ones retrieved, trusted and quoted.

Passage-level extraction

Engines lift short, self-contained passages — not whole pages. Every key claim on your site should live in a clean, quotable block that makes sense on its own.

Semantic (vector) matching

Retrieval matches meaning, not exact keywords — your content is compared as embeddings in a vector index. Clear entity naming and consistent terminology make your pages easier to match and retrieve.

Research-backed methods

The Princeton study that coined GEO measured up to a 40% visibility lift in generative answers from citation-focused optimisation — adding quotations, statistics and cited sources.

Sources: GEO: Generative Engine Optimization (Princeton, arXiv 2311.09735) • Google Search Central: AI features and your website • Wikipedia: Generative engine optimization

Answers

AI SEO: frequently asked questions

What is AI SEO, and how is it different from traditional SEO?

AI SEO structures your website and content so AI-powered search experiences — Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity and Copilot — can understand, cite and recommend your brand. It builds on traditional SEO rather than replacing it (Google itself says optimising for AI "is still SEO"), but adds focus on citations, entities and access for AI crawlers instead of only blue-link rankings.

What is the difference between GEO, AEO and LLMO?

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) targets being cited inside generative AI answers; AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) targets being the direct answer in snippets, voice and answer boxes; LLMO refers to optimising for large language models. In practice these overlap heavily and are used largely interchangeably in 2026 — there is no strict academic distinction.

How do I get my content cited in Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT and Gemini?

Publish authoritative, well-structured content that answers specific questions, strengthen your entity and schema signals, and earn brand mentions and citations from other trusted sites. Relevance and off-page mentions are the strongest correlated signals — but no tactic can guarantee a citation.

Does AI SEO hurt my normal Google rankings?

No. AI SEO and classic SEO share the same foundations — quality content, authority, crawlability and E-E-A-T — so doing it well usually strengthens both your organic rankings and your AI visibility.

How long does AI SEO take to show results?

Typically a few weeks to around three months. Established, authoritative domains often gain traction faster than brand-new sites, and AI visibility is an ongoing programme rather than a one-time fix.

Should I block AI crawlers like GPTBot, ClaudeBot and PerplexityBot?

Only if you do not want to be cited. Blocking answer-engine bots removes you from a fast-growing discovery channel, so most businesses should allow them — and each bot needs its own directive in robots.txt.

Do I need an llms.txt file for AI SEO?

No. Google has publicly stated that llms.txt is not required or used for its AI features. It remains unofficial and unsupported by the major engines, so it is not a priority — solid content, schema and crawlability matter far more.

What does an AI SEO audit include?

It reviews AI crawler access (robots.txt for GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot, ClaudeBot and PerplexityBot), structured data and schema, entity clarity, content structure (headings, lists and self-contained answers), topical authority and internal linking, off-page brand signals, and how you currently appear across AI answers.

How do you measure AI SEO success?

Through AI-visibility tracking of mentions and citations across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google and Gemini, share of voice in AI answers, referral traffic from AI tools, and branded-search lift — not rankings and clicks alone.

Which AI platform matters most for businesses in India?

Google AI Overviews and Gemini have wide reach in India, helped by Android defaults, while ChatGPT and Perplexity matter for research-heavy queries. We optimise for the whole mix rather than betting on a single engine.

Why is my business not showing up in ChatGPT or Perplexity answers?

Usually one of four things: weak entity signals (the engine is not sure who you are), content that is not structured for extraction, few third-party mentions and citations, or blocked AI crawlers. An AI SEO audit identifies which of these apply to your site — most are fixable within weeks.

What is retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and why does it matter for SEO?

RAG is how most AI search engines answer: they retrieve the most relevant passages from a live index and generate a response from them, citing their sources. Pages structured into clear, self-contained, quotable passages are far more likely to be retrieved — and cited.
